7. INVESTIGATION WORKFLOW TIPS

Best Practices for Blockchain Investigations:

  • Start with the Transaction Hash: Begin your investigation with the transaction ID (TXID) or wallet address.
  • Use Multiple Explorers: Cross-reference data across different blockchain explorers for accuracy.
  • Track Fund Flow: Use visualization tools like Breadcrumbs or MetaSleuth to map transaction paths.
  • Identify Entities: Use Arkham Intelligence to link wallets to known entities, exchanges, or criminal groups.
  • Monitor Mixers/Tumblers: Watch for transactions through mixing services that attempt to obscure the trail.
  • Document Everything: Export transaction graphs, screenshots, and reports for case documentation.
  • Check Exchange Deposits: Track funds to centralized exchanges where KYC may reveal identities.
  • Multi-Chain Tracking: Follow cross-chain bridges and wrapped tokens across different blockchains.

‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT NOTES FOR LAW ENFORCEMENT

  • Chain of Custody: Maintain proper documentation of all blockchain evidence collected.
  • Timestamps: All blockchain transactions are timestamped - use this for timeline reconstruction.
  • Immutability: Blockchain data cannot be altered, making it excellent forensic evidence.
  • Privacy Coins: Some cryptocurrencies (Monero, Zcash) offer enhanced privacy features that complicate tracking.
  • Legal Considerations: Ensure all investigation methods comply with local cybercrime laws and regulations.
  • Expert Consultation: For complex cases, consider consulting blockchain forensics experts or companies like Chainalysis, Elliptic, or CipherTrace.
